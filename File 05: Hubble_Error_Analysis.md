# Error Analysis of the Hubble Model

## 1. Introduction
The Hubble parameter $H_0$ is regarded in modern cosmology as a fundamental quantity
describing the expansion rate of the universe. However, its derivation is based on
assumptions that are tenable neither physically nor structurally. This chapter shows
why the Hubble model is not a law of nature but an observational artefact arising
from incorrect derivations and inadmissible simplifications.

---

## 2. Hubble Is Based on the Assumption of a Globally Constant c
The Hubble formula:

$$
v = H_0 \cdot D
$$

assumes:

- that the speed of light is globally constant as an observable  
- that the metric of the universe is homogeneous  
- that redshift directly measures velocity  

These assumptions are incorrect.

From File 01 it follows:

$$
c_{\text{local}} = \text{constant}, \quad c_{\text{eff}} = \text{structurally variable}
$$

If $c_{\text{eff}}$ is larger in voids and smaller in clusters, then:

- Redshift measures metric variation, not velocity  
- Distance is not proportional to z  
- Velocity is not proportional to z  

The mathematical foundation of the Hubble model is thereby destroyed.

---

## 3. Hubble Is Based on Homogenisation
Standard cosmology treats the universe as:

- isotropic  
- homogeneous  
- metrically uniform  

This contradicts all observations:

- Voids (stretched metric)  
- Filaments (anisotropic metric)  
- Clusters (compressed metric)  
- Local gravitational fields  
- Structural variations over billions of light-years  

If the metric is not homogeneous, then the relationship:

$$
z \propto D
$$

is not valid.

Homogenisation generates artificial linearity that does not exist in reality.

---

## 4. Hubble Is Not a Law of Nature
A law of nature must:

- apply universally  
- be independent of observational position  
- be independent of structural conditions  
- be reproducible  

The Hubble parameter satisfies none of these conditions:

- It varies depending on direction (anisotropic Hubble tension)  
- It varies depending on measurement method (local vs. CMB)  
- It varies depending on structure (void vs. cluster)  
- It is not reproducible (Hubble tension)  

A value that changes depending on the measurement region is not a law of nature.

---

## 5. Hubble Is an Observational Artefact
The apparent linearity between redshift and distance arises from:

- incorrect interpretation of z  
- assumption of a global c  
- averaging over inhomogeneous structures  
- ignoring metric variation  
- ignoring gravitational slingshot accelerations  
- ignoring voids with $c_{\text{eff}} > c$  

The Hubble relationship is therefore not a physical connection but an artefact of
measurement interpretation.

Formally:

$$
z \rightarrow \text{metric variation}, \quad \text{not expansion}
$$

If the cause is incorrect, the derived quantity is meaningless.

---

## 6. Consequences for Cosmology
If the Hubble model collapses, the following follow:

- no expansion  
- no accelerated expansion  
- no Dark Energy  
- no global cosmic metric  
- no Friedmann equations  
- no ΛCDM interpretation  

All of modern cosmology is based on an incorrect derivation chain:

$$
z \rightarrow v \rightarrow D \rightarrow H_0 \rightarrow \text{Expansion}
$$

If the first step is incorrect, all subsequent steps are incorrect.

---

## 7. Conclusion
The Hubble model is not a law of nature but an observational artefact arising from
the incorrect assumption of a globally constant c and the homogenisation of a
structurally variable spacetime. As soon as voids, clusters, and metric variation
are correctly accounted for, the apparent linearity between redshift and distance
vanishes entirely. The Hubble parameter thereby loses all physical significance.
