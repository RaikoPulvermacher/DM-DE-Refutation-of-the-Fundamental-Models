# New Interpretation of Cosmology

## 1. Core Principle: Gravitation instead of Expansion
Classical cosmology interprets redshift as a consequence of the global expansion
of the universe.  
In the interpretation presented here, the following applies instead:

- Gravitation and metric structure determine the observed effects.  
- Redshift measures metric variation, not expansion.  
- There is no necessity for an expanding universe.

Gravitation is real; expansion is a model.

---

## 2. Metric Variation instead of Dark Energy
Dark Energy is introduced to explain an apparent accelerated expansion.  
In a structural model with variable metric this is superfluous:

- Voids: stretched metric → $c_{\text{eff}} > c$ → higher redshift  
- Clusters: compressed metric → $c_{\text{eff}} < c$ → different travel times  
- Filaments: anisotropic metric → direction-dependent effects  

The observed "acceleration" is a consequence of metric variation, not of an
additional form of energy.

Dark Energy is an interpretation artefact, not a physical entity.

---

## 3. Real Density Distributions instead of Dark Matter
Dark Matter is introduced to compensate discrepancies between observed and
calculated gravitation.  
In a model with real, structural density distributions the following applies:

- Gravitation follows the actual, inhomogeneous matter distribution.  
- Voids, filaments, and clusters create complex potential landscapes.  
- Global averages are physically meaningless.  

The "missing mass" arises from incorrect averages and incorrect models, not from
invisible matter.

Dark Matter is a model gap-filler, not a necessary component.

## 4. 3.3c as Momentary Structural Resolution
The effective speed of light in voids of approximately $3.3c$ can be understood
as the **momentary lowest recognised spacetime structural velocity**:

- $3.3c$ is not a new law of nature, but a current structural marker.  
- With $3.3c$ we "see" spacetime more finely than with purely local $c$.  
- Gravitation thereby becomes structurally visible without introducing new forces.

Analogy:

- Previously: we view the universe like an image at 480p–640p resolution.  
- Taking $3.3c$ into account: we see the same universe in "HD".  
- If we one day find a region with e.g. $4.4c$, the "resolution" increases
  further (2K, 4K, …).

The structural speed of light acts as a dynamic resolution parameter for spacetime.

## 5. Dynamic Re-Calibration instead of Fixed Constants
In this interpretation:

- $c_{\text{local}}$: invariant, locally valid.  
- $c_{\text{eff}}$: structurally variable, observation-dependent.  
- "3.3c": current calibration point, not absolute.

When a region with higher $c_{\text{eff}}$ is discovered:

- the model is not destroyed but re-calibrated.  
- the structural resolution shifts.  
- gravitation and the metric principle remain unchanged.

The universe stays the same – only our "resolution" becomes finer.

## 6. Summary of the New Interpretation
The new interpretation replaces:

- **Expansion** with **Gravitation and Structure**,  
- **Dark Energy** with **Metric Variation**,  
- **Dark Matter** with **real, inhomogeneous density distributions**,  
- **global constants** with **local invariance + structural effective quantities**.

Redshift measures metric, not velocity.  
Hubble, Friedmann, DM, and DE are artefacts of an incorrect, Euler-based
continuum model.  
Spacetime is structural, discrete, and local – and is "resolved" more sharply with
every newly recognised structural speed of light.
