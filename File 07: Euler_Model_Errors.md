# Error Analysis of the Euler Model

## 1. Introduction
The Euler model is a classical continuum model that assumes continuous change,
continuous flows, and continuous derivatives. It is mathematically elegant, but
physically valid only in systems that are genuinely continuous. In real physics –
and in cosmology in particular – this prerequisite is not fulfilled.

The works *TdR – Theory of Realities* (Pulvermacher, DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.18727529)
and *Energy Revolution 8911: Overcoming Euler Resistance*
(Pulvermacher, DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.18778354) show that the Euler model generates
structural paradoxes as soon as it is applied to discrete, emergent, or nonlinear
systems. Precisely these paradoxes also appear in cosmology – and lead there to DM/DE.

---

## 2. The Euler Model Assumes Continuity
Euler is based on:

- continuous functions  
- continuous derivatives  
- infinitesimal changes  
- continuous flows  
- smooth metrics  

These assumptions are not fulfilled in real spacetime:

- Gravitation is local, not continuously distributed  
- Metric is structurally variable (void vs. cluster)  
- Energy distribution is discrete (galaxies, filaments, clusters)  
- Time is locally different (gravitational time dilation)  

The Euler model is therefore unsuitable for cosmological structures.

---

## 3. Euler Generates Paradoxes in Emergent Systems
The Energy Revolution work shows:

- Continuum models generate *apparent* divergences  
- Exponential growth models generate *artificial* acceleration  
- Compound-interest logic is an emergent model, not a physical one  
- Euler derivatives generate "resistance" that is not real  

Transferred to cosmology:

- the apparent "acceleration" of the universe is an Euler artefact  
- the apparent "missing mass" is an Euler artefact  
- the apparent "Dark Energy" is an Euler artefact  

Euler describes growth – not spacetime.

---

## 4. Compound-Interest Models Have Nothing to Do with the Universe
A central point from the Energy Revolution:

> Compound-interest models describe emergent growth, not physical reality.

The Friedmann equations and the Hubble model, however, use:

- exponential scale factors  
- continuous derivatives  
- global growth rates  

This is mathematically identical to compound-interest models.

Consequence:

- Cosmology interprets redshift like a financial model.  
- The universe is treated as though it is "growing".  
- Expansion is a mathematical artefact, not a physical process.

---

## 5. Euler Generates DM/DE as Mathematical Gap-Fillers
When a continuum model is applied to a discrete universe, errors inevitably arise:

- missing gravitation → "Dark Matter"  
- apparent acceleration → "Dark Energy"  
- divergences in the scale factor → "Inflation"  
- missing homogeneity → "cosmological constant"  

These quantities are not physical discoveries, but:

\[
\text{Euler artefacts}
\]

They arise because the model is incorrect – not because the universe contains
mysterious components.

---

## 6. Connection to TdR: Discrete Reality instead of Continuum
The TdR shows:

- Reality is process-based  
- States are discrete  
- Metric is local  
- Time is local  
- Structure determines dynamics  

This makes clear:

- Continuum models such as Euler or Friedmann are unsuitable  
- Derivatives over global quantities are meaningless  
- Expansion is not a physical process  
- DM/DE vanish in a discrete model  

Euler is a mathematical tool – not a physical law.

---

## 7. Conclusion
The Euler model is unsuitable for cosmological applications because it assumes
continuity, homogeneity, and continuous derivatives that do not exist in the real
universe. The paradoxes that arise from this – missing mass, apparent acceleration,
divergences – are identical to the paradoxes that occur in compound-interest models
and other emergent growth models. The works *TdR* and *Energy Revolution 8911* show
that these paradoxes are structural artefacts of the Euler model. In a discrete,
structural spacetime, DM and DE vanish entirely.
